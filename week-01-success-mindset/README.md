# Assignment 1. What is something you believe to be true that most people around you would disagree with?

### Rules

* No "safe" answers.
* Must be your real belief (not copied from internet).
* Minimum 50 words.

**Hint:** What do you believe about career, money, learning, discipline, relationships, health, success, life, tech industry, etc. that most people don't agree with?

## Answer

One thing I believe that many people may disagree with is the saying, **"There is no wrong time to do the right thing."**

I do not completely agree with it because I believe timing matters. There is a time for everything, and even the right action can lose its value or have unintended consequences if it is done too late. For example, telling the truth is the right thing, but if you withhold it until after the damage has been done, you have missed the moment when it could have made a difference.

To me, doing the right thing also means doing it at the right time, not just eventually. Just like the saying, "Making hay when the sun is still shining."

### Truth

# My 2.0 Version – Looking Back from the Future

Five years ago, Michael Okanlawon made a conscious decision to move beyond technical support and build a career centred on cloud infrastructure, automation, and DevOps. Looking back, that decision transformed both his career and the value he delivered.

Driven by a passion for automation, he successfully transitioned into DevOps engineering. He earned certifications in Microsoft Azure, AWS, Docker, Kubernetes, Terraform, and CI/CD technologies, while proving his skills through practical projects rather than certifications alone.

He also published technical articles on LinkedIn and Medium, sharing practical lessons from his learning journey and helping others understand cloud and DevOps concepts.

Today, Michael works as a DevOps Engineer, contributing to enterprise cloud projects, implementing secure CI/CD pipelines, automating infrastructure, and improving deployment reliability. Beyond his daily responsibilities, he mentors aspiring cloud engineers, supports interview preparation, and contributes to the wider technology community by sharing knowledge and encouraging continuous learning.

His journey demonstrated that integrity, consistency, and continuous learning remain the foundation of long-term success. By combining strong technical expertise with effective communication and a willingness to help others, Michael built a career defined not only by professional growth but also by meaningful impact.

# Reflection – Week 1

### Biggest insight I got about myself this week

I realised I perform better when I follow a structured plan instead of trying to figure things out on the go. Once I break tasks into steps and stick to a routine, I become more consistent and productive, even when motivation is low.

### My biggest weakness/loop I noticed

I noticed a tendency to overthink or delay starting tasks when I do not fully understand everything at once. This sometimes slows down execution and makes simple tasks feel heavier than they are.

### One system I will implement from this week (exact habit + time)

Every Sunday at 8:00pm, I will break down the weekly assignment into clear daily tasks (Monday to Thursday), and immediately start the first step for at least 30–45 minutes so I don’t delay execution.
